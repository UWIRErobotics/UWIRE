    Objectives

    This module will:

    • Discuss the historical background of neuroscience
    • Explain local and distributed neural networks
    • Review representational and computational power of each kind of network as an account of intelligence and cognition
    • Examine instances of neural networks

    Readings and Activities

    Mind: Chapter 7

    Readings: Churchland and Sejnowski

    Discussion Questions:

    1. What kinds of knowledge do distributed networks capture well and capture poorly?
    2. Explain what major factors limit distributed networks as a general account of knowledge. How serious are these limitations?
    3. What are the main strengths and weakness of distributed networks as an account of human intelligence? How might the weaknesses be addressed?
    4. Compare and contrast frames and distributed networks as models of concepts. Which model provides the better account? Explain.
